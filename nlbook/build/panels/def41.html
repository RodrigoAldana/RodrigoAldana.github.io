Definition 4.1 (Lyapunov stability)

Consider the autonomous system

\[\dot{x} = f(x), \qquad f(0)=0\]

so that the origin is an equilibrium point.

The equilibrium is said to be stable in the sense of Lyapunov if

\[\forall \varepsilon > 0 \; \exists \delta(\varepsilon) > 0\]

such that

\[\|x(0)\| < \delta \Rightarrow \|x(t)\| < \varepsilon \quad \forall t \ge 0 .\]

1. Geometric meaning

The definition compares two balls centered at the equilibrium.

  • an outer ball of radius \(\varepsilon\)

  • an inner ball of radius \(\delta\)

The property says that if the trajectory starts inside the \(\delta\) ball, it will never leave the \(\varepsilon\) ball.

Thus stability is a confinement property. The trajectory may move inside the \(\varepsilon\) region, but it cannot escape it.

2. The order of quantifiers

The most important part of the definition is the order

\[\forall \varepsilon >0 \quad \exists \delta(\varepsilon) >0 .\]

This means the following.

First the challenger chooses \(\varepsilon\).

Then we must produce a value of \(\delta\) that guarantees the property for that value of \(\varepsilon\).

The dependence \(\delta=\delta(\varepsilon)\) is therefore essential.

If the order were reversed,

\[\exists \delta>0 \quad \forall \varepsilon>0\]

the definition would be impossible to satisfy.

3. What stability does not require

The definition does not require trajectories to approach the equilibrium.

A trajectory may orbit forever around the equilibrium and still be stable.

A typical example is a frictionless pendulum, whose trajectories are closed orbits.

4. Asymptotic stability

An equilibrium is asymptotically stable if

  • it is Lyapunov stable

  • trajectories starting sufficiently close satisfy

\[\lim_{t\to\infty} x(t)=0 .\]

Thus asymptotic stability combines

\[\text{confinement} + \text{convergence}.\]

5. Why the definition requires \(t\ge0\)

The condition

\[\|x(t)\| < \varepsilon \quad \forall t \ge 0\]

means that the trajectory must remain inside the \(\varepsilon\) region for all future time.

If the condition held only for a finite time interval, the trajectory could eventually escape.

6. Interpretation

Lyapunov stability answers the question:

If the system is slightly perturbed from equilibrium, will the state remain close to it?

This notion is fundamental because real systems are never initialized exactly at equilibrium.
